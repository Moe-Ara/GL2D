# 06 — Puzzle Bible

## Principles

- **Diegetic or dead.** Every puzzle element existed in this world before it
  was a puzzle: bells marked tides, sails caught wind, ballast tilted ships,
  clockwork wound lamps. No keys, no abstract switches, no inventory.
- **Readable in silhouette.** If the interactive shape can't be identified in
  a black-on-grey thumbnail, redesign the shape, not the tutorial.
- **One language per chapter,** introduced safely, developed once, paid off
  once, then retired. Languages may *duet* at act climaxes (bells + Undertow)
  but never triple.
- **Failure is soft.** No death from puzzles; wrong attempts re-flow to the
  start of the attempt, and the environment absorbs the retry (tide sighs
  out; sail luffs; timeline re-freezes).
- **Puzzles are scenes.** Each has a narrative sentence it proves. If cutting
  a puzzle wouldn't lose story, cut it.

## The six puzzle languages

### L1 — Tide Bells (Ch1, reprise Ch4)
*Sentence proved: the sea listens.*
Bronze harbor bells each carry an engraved depth-line. Ringing one (Call
nearby, or striking via counterweights) raises the Ghost Tide within that
bell's basin to its line, for the duration of the note's decay. Tide floats
skiffs/debris (buoyancy), carries Mara (swim), and sinks on decay.
- **#1 First Bell (1.2):** one bell, one skiff, one crossing. Teach.
- **#2 Bell Chorus (1.4):** three terraced basins, three pitches; ride tides
  between terraces; the cracked bell must be struck by swung counterweight
  (mallet-verb via environment). Develop: sequencing + a non-vocal ring.
- **#7 Bell vs. Undertow (4.3):** ringing lifts a wreck for passage *and*
  summons the hunter — ring the far decoy bell, then the near one, pass under
  the hull while the Undertow investigates the decoy. Payoff: the safe tool
  from Act 1 becomes a wager. (Duet with L4.)

### L2 — Mirror Reading (Ch2)
*Sentence proved: the storm never ended; it's just not "now".*
Rain pools reflect the paused storm-night. The reflection is *information*:
reflected lightning casts shadows of mast-tops that the real sky cannot cast.
- **#3 The Trawler's Route (2.2):** cross a salt-dune maze by following
  reflected mast-shadows visible only near pools. Walking through a pool
  ripples it blind for seconds (cost of haste). No fail state — wrong branches
  loop back with a new pool. Single instance; the language is fragile and
  precious, so it is not milked.

### L3 — Wind & Sail (Ch2)
*Sentence proved: the fleet was alive once; its tools still work.*
Wind is constant and directional; cloth catches it; caught wind becomes force
(GL2D force generators on hinged/roped bodies).
- **#4 The Capsized Hull (2.3):** re-hang a boom (push/pull + reach), cut a
  stuck halyard (reach at height), sail fills, hull rolls aside on its keel
  arc. One-step physics with a theatrical result. Reprise-free — wind returns
  only as ambience until the finale's sails-at-rest.

### L4 — Calm Light (Ch4)
*Sentence proved: fear makes the dark bigger.*
The jelly-lantern's radius maps to Mara's steadiness (walk = bright, run/fall
= dim, Call = flare then debt). Anemone stands hold the jelly as fixed lights.
The Undertow cannot cross bright light and hunts sound/panic.
- **#6 Anemone Relay (4.2):** cross a dead-dark gallery by planting the jelly
  ahead, returning through its light, leapfrogging. Distances tuned so a calm
  walk *exactly* covers the dark gap — running (panic) literally shrinks the
  bridge. The mechanic is the theme.
- **(with L1) #7 Bell vs. Undertow** — see above.

### L5 — Ballast Tilt (Ch5)
*Sentence proved: the ship still remembers being at sea.*
Calling in a flooded bulkhead raises Ghost Tide inside the hull; water mass
tilts the wreck between two authored list states (port 15° / starboard 12°).
Everything loose obeys: furniture, chandelier, doors, a piano.
- **#8 Ballroom Tilt (5.2):** flood port hold → list swaps → slid furniture
  stacks against the mezzanine wall = staircase. Then the return corridor
  requires the *original* list — re-flood starboard, and the corridor's
  hazard (a loose piano) becomes the doorstop that holds a bulkhead open.
  Two states, two rooms, one mass — the whole chapter's traversal is one
  seesaw understood deeply rather than many switches understood shallowly.

### L6 — Time Stubs (Ch6)
*Sentence proved: the world is one instant, and she can forgive it forward.*
In the wave's shadow, frozen objects can be **released** (Call, aimed by
proximity) to complete their arrested motion exactly once, permanently, with
one second of true storm-audio. Physics plays honestly; the result re-freezes.
- **#9 The Cart (6.1):** release a mid-roll cart; it finishes rolling to the
  wall = step up. Teach: release is one-way.
- **#10 The Street of Bells (6.2):** three releasables (awning-debris, leaning
  mast, town bell) where **order matters**: mast released first spans the
  gap but blocks the stair unless the awning's debris has already filled the
  pothole that re-angles the mast's fall; the bell's swing knocks the final
  shutter open only after the mast's impact has cracked its mount. Solvable
  by observation (all consequences visible as leaned/aimed masses); wrong
  orders leave a walk-back path and a re-freeze reset shrine (the game's only
  puzzle-reset affordance, diegetic as a small tide-shrine).
- **#11 The Weights (6.3):** inside/outside duet — the lighthouse's third
  counterweight can only be driven by releasing a collapsing beam *outside*
  the window so its fall lands on the weight platform: the storm itself winds
  the lamp. Payoff line of the whole system.

## Micro-interactions (not puzzles, kept under 10 s each)

Push a crate, hold a shutter, part bead-rain, lift the jelly to read a name.
Budget: ≤ 2 per scene, and every one must feed atmosphere or foreshadowing.

| Puzzle | Ch | Type | Est. time | Failure cost |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| #1 First Bell | 1 | teach | 1–2 min | none |
| #2 Bell Chorus | 1 | develop | 4–6 min | re-ring |
| #3 Trawler's Route | 2 | navigate | 4–6 min | loop-back |
| #4 Capsized Hull | 2 | physics | 3–4 min | none |
| #5 Lure Chains | 3 | route light | 5–7 min | chain decay |
| #6 Anemone Relay | 4 | nerve | 4–6 min | re-light walk |
| #7 Bell vs. Undertow | 4 | wager | 5–7 min | checkpoint |
| #8 Ballroom Tilt | 5 | spatial | 6–8 min | re-tilt |
| #9 The Cart | 6 | teach | 1 min | none |
| #10 Street of Bells | 6 | ordering | 7–10 min | shrine reset |
| #11 The Weights | 6 | synthesis | 4–6 min | re-release n/a (retry beam timing) |

Peak cognitive load is #10; peak *emotional* load is #11 — deliberately easier
than #10 so the climax is never gated on cleverness.
