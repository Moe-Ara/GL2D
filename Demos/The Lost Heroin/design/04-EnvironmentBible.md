# 04 — Environment Bible

Spaces, not assets. Each location lists: mood, scale, lighting, weather, layer
plan (FG/MG/BG), silhouette, camera composition, ambient sound, environmental
storytelling, landmarks. All layer plans map onto GL2D's parallax factors
(0 = pinned sky … 1 = world-locked … >1 = foreground).

Global rule: **the Standing Wave is present in the deep background of every
exterior**, growing from a 2-px glint (Prologue) to sky-filling (Ch6). It is
the game's compass, progress bar, and promise.

---

## E0 — The Stilt-House (Prologue)

- **Mood:** hushed, tilted, "the morning after something no one explains".
- **Scale:** intimate — one room, one porch, one under-structure. Ceilings low;
  Mara ~1/3 screen height (the largest she ever is).
- **Lighting:** grey pre-dawn through one window; one cold lamp; interior
  shadows soft, no hard sources.
- **Weather:** none; dead air (curtain hangs mid-sway — first frozen tell,
  unreadable as such on a first playthrough).
- **FG:** window frame, chair-back, door jamb (occlusion cuts as she passes).
  **MG (play plane):** floor, furniture, door, porch, ladders. **BG:** through
  the window then porch — mud flats, hanging kelp lines, horizon (factors
  ~0.85 / 0.5 / 0.15 / sky 0.05).
- **Silhouette:** tilted rectangles; one strong diagonal (the 4° list) that the
  whole composition leans on.
- **Camera:** tight (zoom 1.3×), slow damping; the porch exit executes the
  game's first zoom-out (1.3× → 0.8× over 4 s).
- **Ambient:** room tone, wood creak on her steps, no exterior sound at all
  until the door opens (then: wind with no sea in it — wrongness by absence).
- **Storytelling:** stopped clock 3:12; cold cup; child's boots by the door
  (she puts them on = the "new game" moment); no adult boots.
- **Landmarks:** the door of grey light; the ladder shaft.

## E1 — Brinehollow (Ch1)

- **Mood:** interrupted domesticity; a place that stopped mid-sentence.
- **Scale:** medium; boardwalk lanes 2–3 storeys above the mud; verticality
  between porch level and seabed level used constantly.
- **Lighting:** overcast key, ambient occlusion under the houses; interiors
  glimpsed as darker rectangles with single readable props.
- **Weather:** still air, occasional low salt-mist ribbon at mud level.
- **FG (1.1–1.3):** pilings, railings, hanging nets, laundry lines (occlusion).
  **MG (1.0):** boardwalks, ladders, roofs, harbor basin, bells. **BG:**
  village depth (0.8), mud flats + kelp lines (0.45), wave-glint horizon
  (0.12), sky (0.05).
- **Silhouette:** thin-legged houses like wading birds; bell gantries as
  gallows-curves; ladders everywhere (the village reads as "lines down").
- **Camera:** medium (1.0×), gentle look-ahead along boardwalk direction; drop
  to basin triggers a wide preset.
- **Ambient:** rope creak, loose shutter knock (sparse, spatialized), wind
  through pilings tuned like breath over a bottle; her steps on wood = the
  loudest thing.
- **Storytelling:** meals for three interrupted; a toy boat on a bed; chalk
  height-marks on a doorframe; every hearth cold; footprint river below.
- **Landmarks:** the harbor-basin bowl; the great mole; the three oyster
  terraces; the cracked bell.

## E2 — The Salt Meadows (Ch2)

- **Mood:** blinding, sacred emptiness; agoraphobic wonder.
- **Scale:** vast horizontal; smallest Mara of the game (down to ~20 px in the
  widest shots); dunes give occasional shelter-scale pockets.
- **Lighting:** high-key white; shadows pale blue and long; heat shimmer.
  Mirror pools double every silhouette upside-down.
- **Weather:** wind — constant, visible as salt-dust ribbons and grain-streams
  off dune crests; gusts as physical pushes.
- **FG (1.15):** blown salt streamers, occasional black wreck-timber stakes.
  **MG (1.0):** flats, pools, dunes, wreck hulls. **BG:** dune bands (0.6 /
  0.4), the fleet's far masts (0.3), wave-line now clearly a silver ridge
  (0.1), storm-reflection sky *in pools only*.
- **Silhouette:** horizontals broken by mast diagonals; boat ribs like
  ribcages; Mara the only vertical for minutes at a time.
- **Camera:** widest sustained zoom of the game (0.65×); slow damping;
  pools sequence locks Y to keep reflections composed.
- **Ambient:** wind with grain-hiss; pool ripples; *reflected* thunder — a
  thunder that sounds underwater, only near pools (mystery in audio).
- **Storytelling:** frozen gull far overhead; boats face homeward; salt
  crystallized over a dropped shawl; the cistern shrine with cup of salt.
- **Landmarks:** the trawler keel (mirror puzzle), the fleet graveyard canopy
  of sails, the cistern.

## E3 — The Coral Forest (Ch3)

- **Mood:** cathedral hush → living aurora.
- **Scale:** vertical; canopy 4–6 screens tall; interior grove reads as a
  nave.
- **Lighting:** grey-pink bone light in the dead orchard; then biolume —
  teal/violet emissive chains as the *only* light source in the grove's heart
  (HDR bloom moment); light literally propagates as gameplay.
- **Weather:** spore-drift (dust motes), slow "marine snow" falling upward
  occasionally (tell: gravity is polite here, not law).
- **FG (1.2):** fan-coral blades (big occluders for reveal shots). **MG:**
  climbable trunks, polyp clusters, gates, rope-kelp. **BG:** coral depth
  bands (0.7 / 0.5), pale cliff (0.3), wave now a wall (0.1).
- **Silhouette:** antler-trees; gates as furled anemone-mouths; lure-fish as
  tiny comets. Climbable coral branches, dead ends bulbous (silhouette-as-UI).
- **Camera:** vertical dead-zone opened tall; auto-zoom pulses on Call
  (1.0×→1.08× breathing with the hum); cliff scene locks camera for the
  Warden.
- **Ambient:** coral ticks and clicks (contracting), polyps chiming faintly
  when lit (pentatonic, so chains play melodies by accident), wind above the
  canopy only.
- **Storytelling:** buoy-lanterns strung by humans through coral = an old
  walking path *under* the sea (they lived with it); all lamps dark until her.
- **Landmarks:** first glowing polyp; the grove heart; the Warden cliff; the
  Procession Road below.

## E4 — The Trench (Ch4)

- **Mood:** pressure; the dark is a substance, not an absence.
- **Scale:** oppressive verticals; ceilings of overhang; the sky is gone for
  40 minutes (its return is the chapter's reward).
- **Lighting:** near-total dark; the jelly-lantern is a warm 2-m sphere;
  anemones give cool waypoint pools; the Undertow is *blacker than the
  background* with a debris-glitter edge (visible darkness).
- **Weather:** silt-fall from every touch; cold water-haze layers.
- **FG (1.25):** rock teeth and wreck ribs sweeping past as hard occluders.
  **MG:** trench floor, wrecks, chains, anemone stands. **BG:** faint
  far-wall texture (0.5), void (0.0) — deliberately the thinnest parallax of
  the game; depth is done with light, not layers.
- **Silhouette:** Mara + halo against nothing; wreck ribs like whale
  skeletons; the chain stair a single bright diagonal in the final shot.
- **Camera:** tightest zoom (1.35×), high damping (dark needs stability),
  micro-shake only when the Undertow is near (proximity-driven, subliminal);
  chase reverses look-ahead upward.
- **Ambient:** her breath (first time audible), silt hiss, metal groans with
  physical sources, the Undertow as a *withdrawal* of ambient sound plus felt
  sub-bass — it is heard as a hole in the mix.
- **Storytelling:** the fleet's wrecks with home-facing bows; rescue-boat
  wrecks among them; the father's name-board; lanterns of the procession
  dropped at the mouth — they went in unlit.
- **Landmarks:** the jelly colony; the skylight room; the lifted-hull bell
  gauntlet; the anchor-chain stair.

## E5 — The *Aurelia* (Ch5)

- **Mood:** grand and grieving; a palace that failed its guests.
- **Scale:** monumental exterior (hull fills the background for two scenes);
  interiors human-scale but tilted 15° — the list is the level design.
- **Lighting:** grey shafts through portholes and skylights; dust in every
  beam; one warm cabin (the drawing) as the ship's single warm note; the
  ballroom chandelier as a dead constellation.
- **Weather:** interior rust-fall ("rain" of flakes when the hull groans);
  exterior wind.
- **FG (1.2):** railings, davits, mullions; interior door frames for
  diorama-framing. **MG:** decks, corridors, ballroom floor/furniture (the
  furniture *moves* with tilt — mid-layer physics). **BG:** hull cliff (0.6),
  trench lip and salt haze (0.3), the Standing Wave over the bow (0.08 —
  huge and near).
- **Silhouette:** the funnel's broken crown; chandelier's branching black
  star; the bowsprit line leading the eye to the wave.
- **Camera:** interior scenes allow the list — camera holds 0° while the world
  is drawn tilted (never rotate the camera; the tilt must belong to the ship);
  bow scene runs the game's longest zoom-out.
- **Ambient:** deep hull tones (whale-like — rhyme with the Warden), crystal
  chandelier chimes on tilt, papers settling, her steps suddenly loud on
  parquet; reverb long everywhere except carpeted cabins (dry = intimate).
- **Storytelling:** LANTERNQUAY luggage tags; a corrected route board; dinner
  service mid-plating; the drawing + keychain cabin; empty davits.
- **Landmarks:** hull-face climb; the ballroom; the warm cabin; the bow.

## E6 — Lanternquay under the Standing Wave (Ch6)

- **Mood:** sacred and terrible; a town inside a held breath.
- **Scale:** the wave owns the sky — a glass overhang from horizon to
  horizon; streets are intimate beneath it (biggest scale contrast of the
  game in a single frame).
- **Lighting:** storm-green cathedral light filtered *through* the wave;
  hanging rain-beads catch it as static glitter; released objects flash true
  storm-white for the duration of their motion (lightning exists only inside
  releases); ghost-lights warm the town gradually as the finale nears.
- **Weather:** all weather is *frozen*: rain as bead-curtains, flags rigid,
  smoke columns solid like glass sculpture. Only Mara (and what she releases)
  moves — she trails the world's only ripples and dust.
- **FG (1.2):** frozen spray, bead-rain curtains (she parts them — beautiful
  occlusion). **MG:** streets, carts, the mast, the bell, lighthouse rock and
  interior. **BG:** town depth (0.7), the wave's face (0.15, slow — it must
  feel planetary), figures *inside* the wave suggested only as shadow (0.15,
  never detailed).
- **Silhouette:** the wave's curl = the game's key art shape; the lighthouse
  = the vertical answer to it (composition: question-mark vs exclamation
  mark); the town's roofline reads mid-flinch.
- **Camera:** letterbox engages under the wave (the game's only aspect
  change); slow dolly behaviors; the lamp-room kneel is a locked static;
  the fall sequence uses the slowest tracking of the game.
- **Ambient:** near-silence with pressure (LFE bed); Mara's steps *echo off
  the wave* (long pre-delay slap — sound design's flagship trick); each Time
  Stub release plays one second of full storm audio, hard-gated. The
  lighthouse thaws audio floor-by-floor (wind → rain → clockwork → the name).
- **Storytelling:** everything (this is the answer-chapter): the dog mid-bark
  facing the sea; door-names matching Aurelia tags; her own door with the
  low coat hook; the practice crank; the shadow under the table.
- **Landmarks:** the town bell; the leaning mast; the churchyard gate; the
  Ember Light.

## E7 — Below / Surface (Finale)

- **Mood:** release; grief resolved into weight-less warmth.
- **Scale:** underwater town glowing below; then infinite ordinary sea.
- **Lighting:** lamplight through water — god-rays down, drowned-gold; rising
  lights of the dead like inverted snow; dawn above in rose-grey.
- **Weather:** current-sway on everything (the world finally *moves* in
  unison); at surface: real rain finishing, clearing.
- **Layers:** underwater uses full-depth parallax with light-shaft overlays
  (0.9/0.6/0.3) and particle "marine snow" reversed (rising); surface: sea
  bands (0.5/0.3), sky (0.05), gulls (0.4, animated).
- **Silhouette:** the lighthouse beam as a lighthouse-shaped hole in the
  water's dark; the Warden's full body at last; Mara rising with arms open —
  the poster of the ending.
- **Camera:** buoyant drift-follow (loose damping, slight lag); breaking the
  surface executes the game's final zoom-out into the credits frame.
- **Ambient:** underwater muffle with the lamp's hum; bells swaying softly in
  current; then — surface — the first ordinary seaside morning of the game:
  water lapping, gulls, wind with a sea in it. The absence from the prologue,
  filled.
- **Landmarks:** the burning lamp underwater; the rising lights; the keeper's
  door; the steaming cup (post-credits).
