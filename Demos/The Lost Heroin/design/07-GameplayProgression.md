# 07 — Gameplay Progression

## Verb budget (the whole game)

Movement: **walk, run, jump, climb, drop, swim (Ghost Tide only)**.
Interaction: **reach** (one contextual button: push/pull/hold/touch/lift/kneel).
Voice: **the Call** (one held button).

Seven verbs total. Nothing else is ever added. Progression comes from the
*world* reinterpreting the same verbs, never from new buttons.

## Movement evolution

| Phase | What moves like | Delivered by |
|---|---|---|
| Prologue | careful, small; short hop only | tuned jump ceiling in interiors |
| Ch1–2 | confident traversal; run unlocked; long drops with landing weight | full platforming kit |
| Ch3 | vertical fluency: climbs, kelp-ropes, swings | climb surfaces + rope segments |
| Ch4 | *suppressed*: running is dangerous (dims the jelly, draws the Undertow) — the player voluntarily slows | Calm Light rules |
| Ch5 | tilted movement: the same walk on 15° lists; slides and uphill effort | slope physics + tilt states |
| Ch6 | normal verbs fail on frozen matter (the world says no) until Time Stubs re-permit them selectively | frozen-body interaction rules |
| Finale | buoyant rise: gravity released entirely, one input = ascend | swim-up in true water |

The arc is deliberate: **gain mastery (P–3), have it taxed (4), re-learn it
tilted (5), lose it to the frozen world (6), transcend it (F).** The player's
relationship to walking mirrors Mara's relationship to her guilt.

## The Call's evolution (one button, five meanings)

1. **Ch1:** rings bells → raises Ghost Tide (external, mechanical).
2. **Ch3:** wakes biolume chains and lure-fish (living things answer).
3. **Ch4:** flares the jelly at a cost (her voice has a price in the dark).
4. **Ch5:** raises tide *inside* the ship (her voice moves a thousand tons).
5. **Ch6:** releases time itself (her voice forgives the moment forward).

Same input, same animation family, escalating meaning — the growth curve of
the entire game lives in one button.

## Mechanics introduced / retired

| Mechanic | In | Out | Why it retires |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tide Bells | 1.2 | 4.3 (final duet) | its trust is spent as a wager, then honored in the finale as ambience |
| Mirror Reading | 2.2 | 2.2 | single-use fragile wonder; repetition would cheapen it |
| Wind & Sail | 2.3 | 2.3 | one theatrical proof; wind stays as atmosphere |
| Lure Chains | 3.2 | 3.4 | darkness eats light-chains — the Trench *is* the retirement |
| Calm Light (jelly) | 4.1 | 4.4 | released in sunlight, on-screen, with dignity |
| Ballast Tilt | 5.2 | 5.4 | the ship is left behind |
| Time Stubs | 6.1 | 6.4 | consumed by the ending itself |

Rule: **every mechanic gets a farewell shot.** Nothing silently disappears;
the player sees each tool laid down (this is a grief game — even mechanics get
goodbyes).

## Difficulty curve

Cognitive difficulty rises P→#10 (Street of Bells) then intentionally *drops*
for the climax. Execution difficulty (dexterity) is nearly flat and low; the
Undertow chase is the only timed-input peak and it is checkpointed at 20-s
granularity. Target: a non-gamer finishes in ≤ 5 h with zero walkthrough
lookups; the intended 4 h is the confident path.

## Curiosity curve (what pulls the player forward, per chapter)

| Ch | Bait on the horizon | Question in hand |
|---|---|---|
| P | the glint | where is the sea? |
| 1 | the wave-ridge; bells deeper in | where did everyone go? |
| 2 | the fleet's masts; storm in the mirrors | why does the reflection storm? |
| 3 | the Warden's departures; the dark road | what walks through air? |
| 4 | any light at all; the hull overhead | what is in the dark with me? |
| 5 | the warm cabin; the wave over the bow | whose ship — and whose town? |
| 6 | the lighthouse; her own door | who was keeping the light? |
| F | the surface | none — release, not curiosity |

The Standing Wave grows in every exterior background: the horizon is the quest
log. No markers, no objectives text, ever.

## Checkpointing & sessions

Checkpoints at every scene seam and puzzle start (see doc 10); silent
autosave. Four natural session breaks (act seams P–2 / 3–4 / 5 / 6–F) each end
on a cinematic question so a returning player re-enters on curiosity.
