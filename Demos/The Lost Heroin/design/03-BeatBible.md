# 03 — Beat Bible

Format per beat: **duration | purpose | player emotion | environment | gameplay
| puzzle | transition | cinematic moment | mystery progression**. Feeling-state
changes are marked ⟪STATE⟫ (these are literal FeelingsSystem transitions).

Pacing rule enforced throughout: no two consecutive beats of the same type;
rotation is *explore → wonder → tension → reflect → puzzle → reveal*.

---

## PROLOGUE — The Held Breath (10 min) ⟪STILL⟫

**P1. Waking (3 min)** — Purpose: establish quiet wrongness; teach walk/jump.
Emotion: disorientation. Environment: a child's room in a stilt-house, tilted
4°; grey pre-dawn; a cold cup on the table; a clock stopped at 3:12. Gameplay:
walk, hop over fallen chair, push a door. Puzzle: none. Transition: out to the
porch. Cinematic: the door opens onto *nothing* — where the sea should be, mud
and hanging kelp to the horizon; camera pulls wide and holds 4 s.
Mystery: Q1 opened (*where is the sea?*).

**P2. The Ladder Down (3 min)** — Purpose: teach climb + drop; scale the world.
Emotion: smallness. Environment: under the stilt-house, barnacled pilings like
a forest, boats in mud below. Gameplay: climb down ladders/nets, first
long-drop with landing camera kick. Puzzle: none. Transition: feet touch seabed
(first footstep-in-mud audio — the game's "grass" note). Cinematic: framed
through pilings, the Standing Wave glints once on the horizon (2-px shimmer;
most players won't consciously register it). Mystery: seed of Q1's answer.

**P3. The Footprints (4 min)** — Purpose: give direction without UI. Emotion:
unease. Environment: open mud flats; hundreds of footprints, all one way.
Gameplay: pure walk; run unlocks (input hint appears once, diegetic as wind
pushing her). Puzzle: none. Transition: village silhouette rises ahead.
Cinematic: camera detaches 20% ahead of Mara along the prints (first
look-ahead rail). Mystery: Q2 opened (*where are the people?*).

## CHAPTER 1 — Brinehollow, the Village on Stilts (30 min) ⟪STILL → WONDER⟫

**1.1 Empty Porches (6 min)** — Purpose: environmental storytelling density;
teach the "reach" contextual verb. Emotion: loneliness. Environment: stilt
lanes, laundry, unfinished meals, open doors; everything at porch height —
gameplay is up on the boardwalks with the mud far below. Gameplay:
climb/traverse porch-to-porch, reach-verb to open shutters, touch objects
(touched objects give one soft chord — teaching that touch = voice). Puzzle:
simple route-finding: a collapsed boardwalk forces a descent/re-ascent.
Transition: a bell tolls once, un-rung, deeper in. Cinematic: dolly past a
table set for three through a window (foreground occlusion shot). Mystery:
footprints all originate here; meals interrupted at the same moment.

**1.2 The First Bell (8 min)** — Purpose: introduce the Call + Ghost Tide.
Emotion: discovery. Environment: harbor basin — a dry bowl ringed by mooring
posts, one bronze tide-bell on a gantry. Gameplay: the Call unlocks (held
input; Mara hums; screen-space ripple). Humming near the bell rings it; the
bell's note makes the **Ghost Tide** visible: translucent water rises in the
basin to the bell's engraved line, floating a skiff. Puzzle #1 *(Tide Bells)*:
ring the bell, board the floating skiff, pole to the far mooring. Tide sinks
when the note decays — timing, gentle. Transition: gate to the mole. Cinematic:
first Ghost Tide rise — bloom up, biolume flecks, audio muffles ⟪WONDER for the
duration of the tide⟫. Mystery: Q3 opened (*why does it answer her?*).

**1.3 The Mole (6 min)** — Purpose: quiet reflection after the discovery beat.
Emotion: melancholy calm. Environment: long stone breakwater, bollards, a
line of dropped shoes; the name-board of a fishing boat — *first appearance of
the father's boat-name*. Gameplay: pure walk, one push-block (crate to reach
mole top). Puzzle: none. Transition: mole ends in a fallen span; descend to
the flats. Cinematic: static wide, Mara tiny, walking the full screen-width of
the mole in silhouette against grey sky — poster shot #1. Mystery: boat-name
planted.

**1.4 Bell Chorus (10 min)** — Purpose: first real puzzle chain; cement the
tide language. Emotion: engaged, then wonder. Environment: the oyster-farm
terraces — three stepped basins with rope-and-pulley gantries, three bells of
different pitch. Gameplay/Puzzle #2 *(Tide Bells II)*: each bell floats its own
basin's tide to a different line; Mara must ring, ride, and re-ring across
terraces; one bell is cracked (dead note) and must be *struck* by swinging a
counterweight into it — teaching that the world can substitute for her voice.
Transition: top terrace opens to white distance. Cinematic: on the last bell,
all three basins hold tide at once for 10 s — the village briefly looks alive;
then the tide sighs out together ⟪back to STILL⟫. Mystery: bells stamped with
the lighthouse foundry mark (planting Lanternquay's reach).

## CHAPTER 2 — The Salt Meadows (30 min) ⟪WONDER⟫

**2.1 White Silence (7 min)** — Purpose: palette smash-cut; awe through scale.
Emotion: wonder edged with exposure. Environment: blinding salt flats, heat
shimmer, wind ribbons of salt-dust, sky and ground nearly the same white.
Gameplay: walking; wind gusts physically push/assist (first environmental
force). Puzzle: none. Transition: dark speck ahead resolves into wrecked
fishing boats. Cinematic: camera zooms *out* until Mara is 20 px tall —
loneliness-by-composition; the Warden's shadow crosses the ground once (the
whale itself unseen). Mystery: a distant gull hangs frozen mid-air — first
time-stop tell (unremarked).

**2.2 The Mirror Pools (9 min)** — Purpose: plant the paused-storm truth.
Emotion: uncanny beauty. Environment: shallow rain pools mirror the sky — but
the *reflection shows a storming night sky*, lightning and all, while the real
sky is flat grey. Gameplay: walking through pools (ripples kill the reflection
locally, it heals behind her). Puzzle #3 *(Mirror Reading)*: a wrecked trawler
lies keel-up; its route home is only visible in reflection — masts in the
reflected storm-light cast shadows that point the way through a salt-dune
maze; the player navigates by reading the pools. Failure is soft (dunes loop
back). Transition: dunes end at the fleet graveyard. Cinematic: one lightning
flash *in the reflection only*, whole screen's mirrored half flickers —
poster shot #2. Mystery: the reflected sky = the night of the storm, paused.

**2.3 The Fleet on the Salt (8 min)** — Purpose: traversal variety; foreshadow
Ch4–5. Emotion: reverence (a graveyard, not a playground — motion slowed 5%
here ⟪GRIEF tint, 30 s crossings⟫). Environment: fishing boats scattered like
whale bones; sails make canopies; salt has begun burying hulls. Gameplay:
climb hulls, walk yards, slide sails. Puzzle #4 *(Wind & Sail)*: a capsized
hull blocks the way; re-hanging a boom and loosing a sail catches wind and
rolls the hull aside (physics: hinge + wind force). Transition: land beyond
the fleet turns grey-pink — coral on the horizon. Cinematic: Mara walks a yard
arm silhouetted between two hanging sails breathing in the wind. Mystery: all
boats face *toward* land — they were coming home.

**2.4 Rest: The Cistern (6 min)** — Purpose: designed quiet; first checkpoint
shrine pattern. Emotion: safety. Environment: a stone rain-cistern, intact,
with a shrine niche (cup of salt, tiny bell) and dry blankets — someone waited
out storms here. Gameplay: sit (hold-verb) — camera settles, ambient drops to
near-silence, one music cue (see doc 09; this is the game's first true music).
Puzzle: none. Transition: leaving the cistern, coral fingers rise around the
path. Cinematic: sitting shot holds as long as the player likes; wind ribbons
loop; the Warden passes far in the background sky, first clear sighting.
Mystery: none — breathing room is the content.

## CHAPTER 3 — The Coral Forest (35 min) ⟪STILL → WONDER, ends STILL⟫

**3.1 Bone Orchard (8 min)** — Purpose: verticality debut. Emotion: hushed
curiosity. Environment: dead coral like petrified beech forest, grey-pink,
tall; light shafts through fan-coral canopy; hanging nets and buoy-lanterns
(all dark). Gameplay: full climbing kit (ledges, vines-as-kelp, rope swings).
Puzzle: traversal-as-puzzle: reading climbable coral by silhouette (climbable
= branching, dead-end = bulbous). Transition: a hum-responsive polyp glows
when she Calls near it. Cinematic: first polyp light answering the Call —
close-up insert (auto-zoom 1.4×, 2 s). Mystery: light answers her voice —
Q3 deepens.

**3.2 The Lit Path (10 min)** — Purpose: core Call mechanic matured. Emotion:
wonder (peak of the game's beauty budget until the finale). Environment: living
biolume grove — teal/violet; light propagates polyp-to-polyp in chains.
Gameplay/Puzzle #5 *(Lure Chains)*: Calling starts a light-chain that runs
along coral branches; chains open polyp-gates (they furl when lit) and wake
sleeping lure-fish that follow the newest light; Mara routes chains so a
lure-fish swims (through air, like everything sea-born here) into a dark
hollow and lights it from inside. Two chained instances, second adds a timing
element (chains decay). Transition: grove dims as coral thins toward a cliff.
Cinematic: full-screen chain cascade when the first gate opens — poster #3
⟪WONDER⟫. Mystery: sea-creatures move through *air* — the water never really
left.

**3.3 The Warden (5 min)** — Purpose: act-one curtain; awe. Emotion: awe.
Environment: cliff-edge clearing above a white drop; wind. Gameplay: walk to
the edge (forced slow-walk zone). Puzzle: none. Cinematic: the **Warden**
rises past the cliff face — a whale the height of the screen, air-swimming,
one eye passing Mara at her exact eye level, 12 s, no music until the eye
passes (single low horn note). It banks toward the Standing Wave and shrinks
to the horizon. Camera returns control with Mara unmoved. Transition: a
switchback path down the cliff into shadow. Mystery: the Warden ignores
gravity's rules exactly like the Ghost Tide — the world is one held thing.

**3.4 Descent & The Procession Road (12 min)** — Purpose: pivot to darkness;
answer "where did the people go" halfway. Emotion: growing dread ⟪STILL,
draining toward FEAR at the end⟫. Environment: the path down joins a broad
seabed road scattered with dropped belongings — shoes, lanterns (dark), a
doll, a wheelbarrow — a procession's litter thinning into a black canyon
mouth: the Trench. Gameplay: walking, small climbs; the Call's light-echo gets
visibly weaker as darkness deepens (teaching the Trench rule before it
matters). Puzzle: none — dread needs no tasks. Transition: the canyon swallows
the sky. Cinematic: last light: Mara turns (player-initiated or not) and the
Standing Wave is *visibly closer* than geography allows. Camera does nothing
special — the wrongness is the shot. Mystery: Q2 half-answered: they all
walked *this way*.

## CHAPTER 4 — The Trench (40 min) ⟪FEAR⟫

**4.1 The Jelly Lantern (8 min)** — Purpose: arm the player with fragile
light; invert the power fantasy. Emotion: focus. Environment: trench mouth —
overhangs, columns; a grounded jellyfish colony, one small jelly pulsing
faintly. Gameplay: the reach-verb lifts the jelly — it becomes her lantern,
held overhead; **its brightness = her calm**: walking steady brightens it,
running/falling/panicking dims it (this is the FEAR feeling made mechanical
and diegetic; no meter). The Call temporarily super-brightens it but costs a
long dim after (risk/reward). Puzzle: light-radius route-finding. Transition:
first Undertow sighting far below — a ribbon of blacker-than-black crossing a
gap. Cinematic: lifting the jelly: the first *player-owned* light of the game.
Mystery: something down here moves.

**4.2 The Undertow (12 min)** — Purpose: the tension peak of the mid-game.
Emotion: dread → fright. Environment: trench floor: silt that puffs and hangs,
wreck ribs, anemone stands that glow when the jelly nears (breadcrumb light).
Gameplay: stealth-by-calm: the Undertow (a current of black water with debris
in it) patrols; it senses running and the dark; standing still with a bright
jelly is safe; three route-past setpieces, escalating. No fail-kill on first
contact: it *takes the jelly's light* first (world drops to near-black,
heartbeat, re-light at an anemone) — second contact resets to checkpoint via a
swallowed-by-current smash-cut. Puzzle #6 *(Anemone Relay)*: a dead-dark gallery
crossable only by planting the jelly in anemone stands to leapfrog light.
Transition: a listing wreck bridges upward. Cinematic: the Undertow passes
*over* a glass skylight above Mara — the room darkens, silt falls, it moves
on. Poster #4. Mystery: the fleet lies here; one wreck's name-board is the
father's boat (readable if the player raises the jelly to it — optional,
devastating, missable by design).

**4.3 The Fleet Floor (10 min)** — Purpose: grief planted inside fear; vary
tension with sorrow. Emotion: mourning under pressure ⟪FEAR with GRIEF pockets
(zones around each wreck)⟫. Environment: the fishing fleet, sunk and settled,
nets full of dark bell-buoys; personal effects fused into silt. Gameplay: a
gauntlet mixing anemone relays with the first bells since Ch1 — ringing a
wreck's bell makes its Ghost Tide *briefly* lift the hull for passage
underneath, and the sound draws the Undertow: ring-and-run structure. Puzzle
#7 *(Bell vs. Undertow)*: sequence two bells so the Undertow is drawn to the
decoy while Mara passes the lifted hull. Transition: a chain-and-anchor stair
rises toward a monstrous silhouette overhead: the Aurelia's hull. Cinematic:
under the lifted trawler: its whole keel passes over Mara's held light.
Mystery: outbound rescue boats lie *among* the fleet — they went out in the
storm; nothing came back.

**4.4 Out of the Dark (10 min)** — Purpose: earned relief; retire the jelly.
Emotion: relief → resolve. Environment: climbing the anchor chain out of the
trench shadow into grey light; the Undertow circles below like a moat.
Gameplay: vertical climb setpiece; halfway, the Undertow surges up the chain —
short scripted chase upward (the only chase in the game), ends when Mara
breaks into daylight: the Undertow recoils from open sky. She sets the jelly
in a sunlit pool — it pulses once, released (mechanic retired with dignity).
Puzzle: none. Transition: the chain tops out on the Aurelia's fouled anchor.
Cinematic: emerging from trench-dark to grey daylight = the game's biggest
single lighting cut; held wide shot of the liner above ⟪FEAR releases to
GRIEF⟫. Mystery: the Aurelia was *arriving* — her bow faces the land.

## CHAPTER 5 — The Silent Fleet: the *Aurelia* (30 min) ⟪GRIEF⟫

**5.1 The Hull Garden (6 min)** — Purpose: reset breath; scale. Emotion:
solemn awe. Environment: the liner's exterior — a cliff of riveted steel,
anemones in portholes, a ladder of scupper holes and rivet seams. Gameplay:
exterior climb; rust flakes fall with each grab (touch = consequence).
Puzzle: route-reading. Transition: entering through a shattered ballroom
window. Cinematic: silhouette climb against the pale hull filling 100% of the
background — poster #5. Mystery: lifeboat davits are empty — they *did*
launch; where are they? (Answer already seen: trench floor.)

**5.2 The Ballroom (9 min)** — Purpose: interrupted-lives storytelling at its
densest; introduce tilt. Emotion: intruding on grief. Environment: a grand
ballroom at 15° list — chandelier hanging at wrong angle (all crystal, no
light), instruments mid-song on a small stage, chairs slid to the low wall,
luggage tagged LANTERNQUAY. Gameplay/Puzzle #8 *(Ballast Tilt I)*: Calling in
flooded bulkhead spaces raises Ghost Tide *inside the ship*; flooding the
port hold shifts the list — the chandelier swings, furniture slides, and the
slid-furniture becomes the staircase to the mezzanine. Two-state tilt only
(list left / list right), fully readable. Transition: mezzanine to cabin
decks. Cinematic: the tilt shift is the setpiece: 5 s of groaning steel,
everything sliding in choreographed slow motion, dust and crystal-chime.
Mystery: dinner was being served — the storm rerouting was sudden.

**5.3 The Cabins (8 min)** — Purpose: the father reveal. Emotion: quiet
devastation. Environment: a listing corridor of cabins, each door a diorama
(no interaction needed — windows into lives); one cabin door ajar with a warm
key-light inside (the only warm light on the ship). Gameplay: walking, one
crawl-space; inside the lit cabin: a child's drawing pinned above a bunk —
lighthouse, three figures, wobbly **M**; a keychain with the father's
boat-name. Reach-verb: Mara takes nothing; she touches the drawing; single
held chord; the light in the cabin *goes out softly* (released). Puzzle: none.
Transition: companionway up toward the bow. Cinematic: static shot from inside
the cabin as Mara stands in the doorway, small, haloed by corridor grey.
Mystery: **Q2 closed**: the people of this story were on the water, coming
home, when the light failed (the player can now assemble the failure from the
dead lamps everywhere).

**5.4 The Bow (7 min)** — Purpose: the mid-truth reveal made visual; act
curtain. Emotion: awe/dread braided. Environment: the open bow deck, railing
gone, wind; ahead and above, filling the entire sky: the **Standing Wave** at
close range — a glass cliff with fish and a rowboat suspended inside it.
Gameplay: walk to the bowsprit (forced slow zone; the game's longest
uninterrupted player-controlled "shot"). Puzzle: none. Transition: a fallen
mast bridges from the bow down toward the wave's shadow. Cinematic: **Q1
closed on camera**: zoom-out from Mara on the bowsprit until the wave's curve
reads unmistakably as a *breaking wave*, 10 s hold, single deep sub-bass
note — the loudest sound since the storm ⟪GRIEF, vignette maximum of the
game⟫. Poster #6 and probable box art. Mystery: Q3 is now the only question
left — and the town under the wave is visibly *hers*.

## CHAPTER 6 — The Standing Wave: Lanternquay (35 min) ⟪GRIEF → RESOLVE⟫

**6.1 Under Glass (8 min)** — Purpose: walk into the held second. Emotion:
awe, trespass. Environment: the wave's shadow: town outskirts frozen
mid-instant — rain hanging as beads (walkable-through; they part around her),
a gull nailed to the air, a dog mid-bark, laundry rigid mid-flap; light is
storm-green filtered through the wave overhead. Gameplay: nothing frozen can
be pushed, climbed, or moved — old verbs deliberately fail (each failed push
gives a dead "stone" sound). Then: the Call has changed here — **it releases
time locally, once per object**: the gull finishes one wingbeat; the beads of
rain around her fall (a ring of sudden rain then silence). Puzzle #9 *(Time
Stubs I)*: release a mid-roll cart so it completes its roll to become a step.
Transition: deeper streets. Cinematic: first release — the world's sound
returns for exactly the duration of the motion, then re-freezes; the audio
design *is* the cinematic. Mystery: **the truth is walkable now**; players
who counted stopped clocks know the time: 3:12.

**6.2 The Street of Bells (9 min)** — Purpose: puzzle crescendo; grief made
mechanical. Emotion: sorrowful focus. Environment: Lanternquay's high street
climbing toward the light — the town bell mid-swing, a toppling mast leaning
un-fallen against a roofline, a shopfront awning bulged with un-fallen debris.
Gameplay/Puzzle #10 *(Time Stubs II — routing)*: releases are one-way and
permanent; the street is a small spatial logic chain: release the mast too
early and it blocks the stair; release the awning first and its debris fills
the gap the mast needed — order matters, and every release plays one honest
second of the storm's sound. A releases-counter is never shown; readable
consequences only. Transition: churchyard gate below the lighthouse rock.
Cinematic: releasing the town bell: one full *true* bell toll rolls across the
frozen town — every window flickers ghost-light for a beat. Poster #7.
Mystery: family names on doors match luggage tags from the Aurelia.

**6.3 The Ember Light — the Climb (10 min)** — Purpose: converging interior
climb; thaw the night. Emotion: mounting recognition ⟪GRIEF deepening;
RESOLVE seeded⟫. Environment: lighthouse interior, spiral; each level thaws
*audio only* as she enters: L1 wind; L2 rain on glass; L3 the clockwork
ticking, slowing; L4 a woman's voice from below the stairs, wordless, then
one name — "Mara" (the game's only word); lamp room above. Gameplay: climbing
gated by re-winding: the great clockwork weights must be raised at three
stations (crank-hold; each raise re-lights one storey's ghost-lamps); the
child-height practice crank at station one *is* her handhold — planted truth
becoming mechanism. Puzzle #11 *(The Weights)*: the third weight's rope is
frayed; she must release (Time Stub) a fallen beam mid-collapse *outside* the
window so it completes its fall onto the counterweight — the storm helps her
wind the light. Transition: the lamp room hatch. Cinematic: each audio thaw is
staged as a held landing shot; no music, the house does the scoring.
Mystery: Q3 closing — her coat hook, her crank, her stairs.

**6.4 The Lamp Room (8 min)** — Purpose: the reveal and the choice-shaped
non-choice. Emotion: recognition → tenderness → resolve ⟪RESOLVE⟫.
Environment: the lamp room, cold lamp, run-down clockwork; under the map
table: a small girl-shadow, knees up, hands over ears. Gameplay: the only
mandatory non-traversal input of the game: walk to the table, kneel
(hold-verb), reach. The shadow takes her hand, stands, dissolves into her.
Then the lamp: wick, crank — and the Call, held, fullest version; ghost-lights
rise through the whole town below to lend their glow (every light the player
ever lit is visible rising). The lamp catches. Puzzle: none. Transition: light
floods the frame → Finale. Cinematic: the kneel is shot static, profile,
letterboxed — the game's one sacred frame. Then the lamp ignition: the beam
sweeps once across the inside of the Standing Wave and the wave *begins to
move*. Mystery: **all questions closed.** The wave may fall now.

---

## FINALE — Lowtide (15 min) ⟪RESOLVE → STILL⟫

**F1. The Fall (4 min)** — Playable: Mara walks the gallery as the wave comes
down in extreme slow motion — not a cutscene; walking is the witnessing. Sound
design: an exhale, not a roar. The screen floods to blue-white.

**F2. Below (6 min)** — Playable, underwater at last: Lanternquay beneath the
sea, the lamp still burning below the surface; the drowned rise past her as
soft lights; the Warden swims by *in water*, immense, and looks at her again.
Mara rises with the lights (swim-up input, effortless; buoyancy carries her).
No puzzle, no threat; the mechanics of the whole game (light, bells ringing
softly in current, tide) recur as farewell images.

**F3. Surface (5 min)** — Dawn on open water; the sea ordinary and everywhere;
gulls (moving). The Ember Light stands above the waterline, turning. Final
playable walk along the gallery into the keeper's door; she hangs her coat on
the lowest hook. Cut to black on the sound of the clockwork, healthy, wound.
Credits over slow drift down through lamplit water. Post-credits single shot:
the cup from the prologue table, steaming.
